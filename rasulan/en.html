    • A Tradition to Show Gratitude and Homage to Ancestors: Rasulan in Dusun Ngasem

    By: Muhammad Fikri Al-Barik (Tim KKN-PPM UGM 2022)

    Welcome to Dusun Ngasem

    I remember the first time I came to Dusun Ngasem. It was at the end of June 2022 and the day was scorching hot. It took approximately 30 minutes from our dormitory to Dusun Ngasem by motorcycle. My sweats were all over the shirt I was wearing on the way to Dusun Ngasem. However, the journey was paid off by the hospitality of the people in Ngasem to me as if they are welcoming their own family.

    Visiting Rasulan Tradition

    After almost three weeks in the village, on July 13th, 2022 specifically, I heard that there will be a huge event held in Dusun Ngasem. Turned out the event is called Rasulan, a term that I have never heard before. Rasulan is a tradition held every year in many regions in Gunungkidul Regency and is also celebrated by people in Ngasem.

    The sun rose in the morning with the joyful news that Rasulan will be held soon. Even though the weather was a bit cold, people in Ngasem were so excited to celebrate Rasulan. The street was full of food stalls and various booths selling toys. The most favorable booth is a fishing booth where kids were able to do fishing the provided fake fish.

    Ledhek

    After a delightful breakfast with Mr. Suwarno, I continued to follow the events of Rasulan by walking around the sub-village. Began with walking to the south through the field that was decorated with tents and pennants to enlive the event. There we could see a beautiful Ledhek dancer who danced supply following the sounds of Gamelan, the traditional instrument of Java.

    Ledhek is the name of Java’s traditional dance that is accompanied by Javanese songs during the dancer’s performance. The thing that makes it different from the other traditional dances is that the songs are requested by the audience. Aside from its aim to entertain the people, Ledhek is also believed to fertilize the farmland so that people will be bestowed with abundant harvests in the years to come.

    Ngrancah Prosession

    The Ledhek dance was over at 1 p.m. The people had gathered around for the next session under a huge tree beside the stage for Ledhek. That big tree is a sacred tree that marks the establishment of Dusun Ngasem.

    The root of the tree looks immense and rooted to the ground. Some of the people there carried a big package containing banana leaves filled with cooked chicken. The chicken that is qualified to be cooked is a chicken that only has one color without any pattern. Furthermore, it is not allowed to be tasted so that before the people are actually eating it together, no one knows how it tastes. This year, the people in Ngasem cooked 25 chicken in total for this celebration.

    After the people were gathered under the tree, the feast called Ngrancah began. The event was started by prayers led by the respected elder of the sub-village. Every person was given three pieces of chicken that were covered with banana leaves. Before we started eating, we were expected to pray and utter our wishes for the coming years, mainly wishes for fertility and prosperity.

    Ngrancah procession occurred for about 30 minutes where the people had to finish their dishes for their wishes to be granted. The feast was concluded with closing remarks from the respected people of Ngasem. The event was continued by Campursari until the evening.

    The Rousing Campursari

    Campursari was held at the field behind Mr. Suwarno’s house. A big stage had been prepared for the Campursari singer to perform. Not only that, Gamelan and the other instruments have been arranged neatly on the stage. When the singer began to sing, I immediately sat in one of the seats provided there. The people also seemed to enjoy the performance from their terrace.

    I felt quite starving after watching Campursari for a while. So, I decided to walk around and visit one of the food stalls. I bought grilled sausage and rainbow syrup shaved ice with only 20.000 rupiahs! The grilled sausage tastes good, but the shaved ice is just bland.

    After strolling around, I came back to the field where Campursari takes place. There were many adolescents dancing along enjoying the performance while holding some money from their pockets. It has been a habit of the people to give a singer some money as a form of thankfulness for the songs. This habit is also called as “sawer”.

    There was a man sitting beside me who did not look like a local. After having a chit-chat with him, I acknowledge that he came from Jogja to Ngasem to watch Rasulan. He also told me that Rasulan exist in his hometown as well but no one continue to perceive this tradition today. By watching Rasulan in Ngasem, he felt very nostalgic. Rasulan is indeed a tradition that should be perceived.

    At 5 p.m, I asked for Mr. and Mrs. Suwarno’s permission to go back to the dormitory. I went back to take a bath and take a rest. Besides, I also prepared to have a sleepover at Mr. Suwarno’s house with my five other friends. The plan to have a sleepover at Mr. Suwarno’s house was due to the peak event of Rasulan. So, the last event was Wayangan which began from 9 p.m to 4 a.m at dawn.

    Wayangan

    We came back to Dusun Ngasem to watch Wayangan at 8 p.m. The full moon was covered by thick clouds that evening. It made the way to Dusun Ngasem quite dark.

    Arrived at Ngasem, the stage was prepared already for Wayangan. There are various sizes of Wayang that are placed on the stage. The crowdy audience has filled the big tent at the sides of the stage. Not only under the tent, people also brought their own mats to sit under the trees that surround the field.

    The wind breezed quite strongly. Yet the cold evening did not discourage the people to watch Wayangan. Shortly afterwards, the “dalang” came to the stage and the Wayang show started.

    During the first half an hour of the performance, everything was fine. However, suddenly it began to rain afterward. People started to seek for a safe place, mostly in the terrace of their neighbors. Even though they were watching while seeking for comfortable places, they still looked enthusiastic to watch Wayang.

    The rain still refused to stop until midnight. The Dalang whose at the beginning was proficiently playing the Wayang took a short break and then replaced by the Campursari singers. Some of the people were seen leaving the field to their houses to take a rest but some of them were staying to watch Wayang until the end.

    I then decided to take a rest as well from this hectic yet unique day. That’s all I can say about our experience during the Rasulan tradition in Dusun Ngasem. Every minute of it was filled with interesting things that I have never experienced before. Every step that I took was full of people’s warmness. If there is a chance in the future to visit Dusun Ngasem again and experience Rasulan one more time, I will be pleased to come back.
