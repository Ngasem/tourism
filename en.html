    The Cliffs

    Dusun Ngasem is a territory that has a line of cliffs, bordering the sea. There are four cliffs in Dusun Ngasem from the east to the west side, namely Kijingan Cliff, Watusigar Cliff, Tutup Cliff, and Pringkuku Cliff. Four of the cliffs have their own uniqueness with exquisite landscapes. The road to the cliff is surrounded by large and fertile fields and farms owned by the Ngasem people. Aside from its appealing view, the cliffs of Ngasem are also used by the people to look for lobsters, shells, crabs, and other commodities. Many people from outside the village also come to the cliffs during the rainy season, mainly to do fishing.

    Rasulan

    Rasulan is an annual tradition that is held in some regions in Gunungkidul Regency, for example in Dusun Ngasem. Rasulan is conducted as a form of gratitude to God for giving a prosperous harvest. Moreover, people also pray to God in order to receive prosperity, health, and welfare. Rasulan is called “Bersih Dusun” as well because it signifies the purpose of this tradition to achieve blessings and ward off calamity so that the people are able to live a good life.

    In Dusun Ngasem, Rasulan is also a ritual to send homage and prayers for ancestors. The time to hold Rasulan must follow the Javanese calendar and must not hold randomly. The events of Rasulan include Ledhek dance, Ngrancah procession, Campursari, and Wayang Kulit. Rasulan in Dusun Ngasem is also enlivened by bazaars and various stalls. During Rasulan, relatives of the people in Ngasem usually visit the neighborhood and people there cook special dishes similar to the Eid Al-Fitr celebration.
